# Hadith Collection: Fajr Time and Dawn

## Primary Hadith: The Two Dawns

> **"الفَجْرُ فَجْرانِ: فَجْرٌ يُحِلُّ الصَّلاةَ وَلاَ يُحَرِّمُ الطَّعامَ، وَفَجْرٌ يُحَرِّمُ الطَّعامَ وَيُحِلُّ الصَّلاَةَ"**

**Translation:** "Dawn is of two types: a dawn that permits prayer but does not prohibit food, and a dawn that prohibits food and permits prayer."

**Source:** Sahih al-Jami` by Al-Albani (2031)  
**Original narrators:** Talaq ibn Ali al-Hanafi  
**Grades:** Hasan (good) by Al-Albani; also in Abu Dawud (2348), Tirmidhi (705), Ahmad

---

## Supporting Ahadith

### 1. Timing of Suhoor
> **"كلوا واشربوا حتى يعترض لكم الشعاع"**  
> "Eat and drink until the (true) dawn appears for you."

**Source:** Sahih al-Bukhari (1841), Sahih Muslim (1091)

**Explanation:** The "شعاع" (beam/radiance) refers to the horizontal spread — true dawn.

---

### 2. Adhan Timing
> **"يُؤذنُ للصلاةِ إذا طلعَ الفجرُ الصادق"**  
> "The Adhan for prayer is called when the true dawn appears."

**Source:** Musnad Ahmad, Sunan Ibn Majah  
**Note:** Most scholars hold that the time for Fajr prayer begins with **true dawn** (al-fajr al-sadiq), not false dawn.

---

### 3. Prohibition of Eating
> **"إذا sawt الفجر، فليمسِكْ عن الطعامِ من أصبحَ صائمًا"**  
> "When you see the dawn (true), then the one fasting should stop eating."

**Source:** Sahih al-Bukhari (2341)  
**Clarification:** Seeing the *true* dawn is the signal.

---

## Scholarly Consensus (إجماع العلماء)

- ** consensus:** Fajr time enters with **al-fajr al-sadiq** (horizontal spread)
- **Method of verification:** Visual observation of eastern horizon (رؤية العين)
- **If horizon obstructed:** Use reliable calculation (الاحتجاز بالحساب)

** führer:** This is why the hadith specifies *two* dawns — to prevent confusion.

---

## Practical Implications

1. **Suhoor must stop** at true dawn (not at false dawn)
2. **Fajr prayer time** starts at true dawn
3. **Fasting validity** depends on stopping at true dawn
4. **Travelers:** Can follow the local true dawn as observed

---

## Appendix: Arabic Text Variants

All versions agree on the two types. Some narrations use:
- "الفجر يطلع ويذهب" (dawn rises then disappears) → false
- "الفجر يطلعل باسطة" (dawn appears spread) → true

**Key phrase:** "حتى يَعْتَرِضَ لَكُمُ الاحْمَرُ" in some narrations refers to the redness of true dawn (al-ihmar).
