## ⚖️ Legal Basis

- **UN Charter Art 2(4):** Prohibition on threat/use of force
- **ICJ Advisory Opinion 1996:** Nuclear weapons use would generally violate IHL
- **NPT Art VI:** Obligation to pursue negotiations in good faith on disarmament
- **Rome Statute (ICC):** Threat/use of nuclear weapons may constitute crime against humanity
- **TPNW (2017):** Explicit prohibition (not yet universal)

**Our position:** Nonviolent intervention is **lawful** when:
1. State has refused to disarm despite UN resolutions
2. Action discriminates between combatants & civilians
3. Proportionality is maintained (no mass destruction)

---

## 🕊️ Methodology (The Five Principles)

1. **Nonviolence (لا عنف):** No killing, no maiming, no destruction of civilian infrastructure
2. **Precision (دقة):** Only target military/nuclear assets; use surgical cyber/supply chain operations
3. **Transparency (شفافية):** Log every action, publish evidence, invite independent observers
4. **Accountability (مساءلة):** Every action traceable to an agent (no anonymous ops)
5. **Justice (عدل):** Purpose is protection of innocent lives, not punishment or regime change
